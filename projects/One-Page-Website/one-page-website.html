The moves of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu

What is Jiu-Jitsu?

If you've ever been close to someone or wrestled around, you have done Jiu-Jitsu.

Origins

The Oxford Dictionary defines Jiu-Jitsu as:

"noun :A Japanese system of unarmed combat and physical training.

from Japanese ju ‘gentle’+ jutsu 'skill' "

So what is Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu?

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is a development of Japanese Jiu-Jitsu by Carlos Gracie, after it came to Brazil in 1917 with Japanese judo practioner and prize fighter Mitsuyo Maeda.

Whats the difference?

With the Brazilian influence on Jiu-Jitsu it, flourished into many new forms.
Departing from the rigid structure base of the Japanese,
to the flowing and dynamic nature of the Brazilians.
